Datacom Market Outlook Revised Higher; Positive for COHR, FN, and LITE

English Version

1. Datacom optical forecast revised higher

LightCounting lifted its datacom optical module forecast. The market is now expected to grow from roughly $19 billion in 2025 to about $47 billion in 2028, implying a CAGR near 35%. The revision is broadly constructive for optical component suppliers in J.P. Morgan's coverage universe, including Coherent, Fabrinet and Lumentum, while visibility for NeoCloud capex remains more uncertain from 2027 onward.

  • 1.6T is the largest growth driver, with an estimated CAGR of roughly 200% and a 2028 market size near $21 billion.
  • 800G is expected to grow at roughly 26% CAGR and also approach $21 billion by 2028.
  • 3.2T begins to contribute modestly in 2028, while 400G and lower-speed modules decline over the forecast period.
  • The forecast was raised by about $9 billion over the covered period, including roughly $5 billion from 1.6T and $4 billion from 800G.

2. Optical attach rates per XPU continue to rise

The report highlights a steady increase in optical content attached to accelerators. The blended optical module ratio was around 2.5x per XPU in 2023, moved to just under 4.0x in 2025, and is expected to approach 4.5x by 2027. Architectures differ materially: Google's OCS-based deployment is closer to 1.5x per TPU, while NVIDIA GPU deployments are closer to 6.0x per GPU.

3. Telecom and DCI outlook trimmed modestly

The telecom and DCI market, including CWDM and DWDM, is now expected to grow from about $4 billion in 2025 to about $6 billion in 2028, a CAGR near 14%. The update is slightly lower than the January view, mainly because the 1.6T ZR and embedded-form-factor outlook was pushed out by roughly one year as customers wait for multi-span amplifier and line-system availability.

  • 1.6T telecom/DCI optics are expected to grow at more than 130% CAGR and reach about $1 billion by 2028.
  • 800G telecom/DCI optics are expected to grow at about 44% CAGR and exceed $2 billion by 2028.

4. OCS market outlook remains solid

The OCS market is expected to expand from about $1.3 billion in 2026 to about $4.3 billion in 2031, implying a CAGR near 26%. Google is identified as a leading adopter through the forecast horizon, using 300-port and 128-port switches to connect up to 9,000 TPUs and to substitute for electrical backplane switching in selected architectures.

The core OCS attributes are high bandwidth, low latency, low power, and the ability to reuse switches across interconnect generations as network topology evolves.

5. CPO forecast likely has further upside

The CPO market is expected to grow strongly, reaching about $2 billion in 2028 and roughly $11 billion in 2030. The report suggests current industry estimates may still be conservative, especially because Lumentum's latest guide implies its own 2028 CPO revenue could approach the current industry TAM estimate for that year.

  • Non-CPO transceivers remain healthy, with the broader receiver/transceiver market expected to grow at about 20% CAGR and exceed $45 billion by 2030.
  • Before 2028, most datacom growth is still expected to come from traditional pluggable modules, which could exceed $40 billion by 2028 at about 30% CAGR.
  • LRO/LPO modules and CPO are both expected to grow rapidly from small bases, each approaching about $3 billion by 2028 under the forecast.
  • The CPO inflection is expected around 2028, driven by scale-up deployments such as dual-rack and multi-rack systems. From 2028 onward, scale-up could account for more than 45% of the CPO market.
  • By 2030, CPO is expected to represent roughly 20% of the total datacom market. At the port level, 800G remains mostly traditional transceivers, 1.6T is more balanced, and 3.2T is expected to be mostly CPO.

Research Read-Through

The report is most supportive of optical component suppliers with exposure to 800G, 1.6T, CPO and datacom transceiver content. It is also relevant to OCS suppliers and optical switching ecosystems, because OCS is framed as a viable topology tool for AI fabrics rather than a purely experimental architecture.

中文翻译

1. 数据中心光模块预测上调

LightCounting 上调了数据中心光模块预测。市场规模预计从 2025 年约 190 亿美元增长到 2028 年约 470 亿美元，复合年增长率约 35%。这总体利好摩根大通覆盖的光器件供应商，包括 Coherent、Fabrinet 和 Lumentum；但报告也提醒，2027 年以后 NeoClouds 的 AI 基础设施资本开支可见度仍然有限。

  • 1.6T 是最大的增长驱动，预计 CAGR 约 200%，到 2028 年市场规模约 210 亿美元。
  • 800G 预计 CAGR 约 26%，到 2028 年同样接近 210 亿美元。
  • 3.2T 预计从 2028 年开始贡献适度收入，而 400G 及更低速率模块将在预测期内下滑。
  • 相比此前预测，整个预测期平均上调约 90 亿美元，其中 1.6T 上调约 50 亿美元，800G 上调约 40 亿美元。

2. 每 XPU 光模块配比持续提升

报告强调，连接加速器所需的光模块配比仍在提升。混合口径下，2023 年约为 2.5 倍/XPU，2025 年升至略低于 4.0 倍/XPU，2027 年预计接近 4.5 倍/XPU。不同架构差异很大：Google 使用 OCS 的方案约为 1.5 倍/TPU，而 NVIDIA GPU 部署约为 6.0 倍/GPU。

3. 电信与 DCI 市场展望小幅下调

电信与 DCI 市场，包括 CWDM 和 DWDM，预计从 2025 年约 40 亿美元增长到 2028 年约 60 亿美元，CAGR 约 14%。这一预测较 1 月口径略有下调，主要原因是 1.6T ZR 和嵌入式外形尺寸的需求节奏被推迟约一年，客户仍在等待多跨段放大器和多跨段线路系统可用。

  • 1.6T 电信/DCI 光模块预计 CAGR 超过 130%，2028 年达到约 10 亿美元。
  • 800G 电信/DCI 光模块预计 CAGR 约 44%，2028 年超过 20 亿美元。

4. OCS 市场前景保持稳健

OCS 市场预计从 2026 年约 13 亿美元增长到 2031 年约 43 亿美元，CAGR 约 26%。Google 被认为将在预测期内保持领先地位，其方案使用 300 端口和 128 端口交换机连接多达 9000 个 TPU，并在部分架构中替代电气背板交换。

OCS 的核心优势包括高带宽、低延迟、低功耗，以及在多个互联世代中复用交换机来适配网络拓扑演进的能力。

5. CPO 预测仍有进一步上调空间

CPO 市场预计将强劲增长，2028 年达到约 20 亿美元，2030 年扩大至约 110 亿美元。报告认为当前行业预测可能仍偏保守，尤其是 Lumentum 最新指引显示，其 2028 年 CPO 收入可能已经接近当前行业对当年 CPO TAM 的估计。

  • 非 CPO 收发器市场仍然稳健，预计到 2030 年以约 20% CAGR 增长，市场规模超过 450 亿美元。
  • 2028 年前，数据中心市场绝大部分增长仍来自传统可插拔模块，预计 2028 年市场规模超过 400 亿美元，CAGR 约 30%。
  • LRO/LPO 模块和 CPO 都将从较小基数快速增长，预测到 2028 年均接近 30 亿美元。
  • CPO 真正的拐点预计出现在 2028 年，主要由双机架、多机架等 scale-up 应用驱动。从 2028 年起，scale-up 可能贡献 CPO 市场 45% 以上。
  • 到 2030 年，CPO 预计占整个数据中心市场约 20%。从端口渗透率看，800G 多数仍由传统收发器提供，1.6T 在传统模块和 CPO 之间相对平衡，3.2T 多数预计由 CPO 提供。

研究映射

这篇报告最直接利好与 800G、1.6T、CPO 和数据中心收发器内容量相关的光器件供应商。同时，报告也强化了 OCS 作为 AI fabric 拓扑工具的可行性：它不再只是实验性架构，而是可以被大规模训练网络吸收的光层重构能力。
